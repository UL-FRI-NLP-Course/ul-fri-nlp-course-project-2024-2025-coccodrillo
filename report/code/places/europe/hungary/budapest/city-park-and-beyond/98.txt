National Institute for the Blind | Budapest, Hungary |  One of the most extravagant art nouveau buildings in Budapest is within easy walking distance of City Park. To the southeast of the park is the National Institute for the Blind, dating from 1904 and designed by Sándor Baumgarten (1864–1928) and Zsigmond Herczegh (1849–1914), with characteristic Oriental and Hungarian art nouveau influences.
